Dr. Edward Said

The Voice of the Voiceless

Edward Said lectures at Columbia University on the impact of Western literature on perceptions of the East - part of his lifelong effort to deconstruct and critique cultural imperialism

Here's a timeline of Dr. Edward Said's life:

Early Life and Education

  • 1935: Born on November 1 in Jerusalem.
  • 1947: Moves to Cairo, Egypt.
  • 1951: Sent to the United States for education.
  • 1957: Graduates from Princeton University (B.A.).
  • 1960: Earns a Master's degree from Harvard University.
  • 1964: Receives a Ph.D. in English Literature from Harvard University.

Academic and Literary Career

  • 1963: Begins teaching at Columbia University.
  • 1966: Publishes "Joseph Conrad and the Fiction of Autobiography."
  • 1978: Releases "Orientalism," his seminal work.
  • 1983: Publishes "The World, the Text, and the Critic."
  • 1984: Becomes Parr Professor at Columbia University.
  • 1993: Releases "Culture and Imperialism."
  • 1999: Publishes his memoir "Out of Place."

Political Activism

  • 1977-1991: Serves on the Palestinian National Council.
  • 1988: Advocates for a peaceful two-state solution.
  • 1991: Resigns from the Palestinian National Council.

Later Life and Legacy

  • 2000: Co-founds the West-Eastern Divan Orchestra.
  • 2003: Dies on September 25 in New York City.

Posthumous

  • 2004: "Humanism and Democratic Criticism" published posthumously.
  • 2006: The Edward Said National Conservatory of Music is named in his honor.
